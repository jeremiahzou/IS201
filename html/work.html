The Perfect Golf Swing





1. The Back Swing
  • Start balanced with pressure in the middle of your feet.

  • Grip relaxed but secure.

  • Take the club away with chest, arms, and hands moving together.

  • Keep clubface square early (not rolling open).

  • Turn shoulders fully until your back faces target.

  • Let hips rotate naturally, but less than shoulders.

  • Shift pressure into trail heel/inside of trail foot.

  • Lead arm stays extended, trail arm folds naturally.

  • Wrists hinge upward by halfway back.

  • Finish at top in balance, loaded, not swaying.
2. Perfect Impact
  • Start downswing from ground up (hips begin first).

  • Shift pressure into lead foot early.

  • Hands drop inside while chest stays closed briefly.

  • Hips open to target.

  • Head stays behind ball.

  • Hands slightly ahead of clubhead (for irons).

  • Strike ball first, then turf.

  • Rotate through impact - don't flip hands.

  • Maintain posture.

  • Balanced, committed acceleration.
3. Perfect Follow Through
  • Extend arms fully after strike.

  • Chest rotates to face target.

  • Belt buckle faces target.

  • Weight finishes almost fully on lead foot.

  • Trail foot up on toe.

  • Club wraps around body naturally.

  • Spine tall and balanced.

  • Hold finish for 3 seconds.

  • No falling backward.

  • Finish like a pro: stable and complete.
